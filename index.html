Paradise in the Eastern Visayas

Leyte province, located in the heart of the Philippine archipelago, is home to some of the country's most stunning and unspoiled coastal destinations. With powdery white sand, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and vibrant marine life, these beaches offer a slice of tropical paradise far from the crowds.

Kalanggaman Island

⚲ Palompon, Leyte

One of the most photogenic islands in the Philippines, Kalanggaman is famous for its breathtaking 795-meter white sandbar that stretches out into crystal-clear waters. Named after the local word "langgam" (bird), its shape resembles a bird in flight. Recognized by Lonely Planet as one of the most picturesque islands in the Philippines, it's a must-visit destination with pristine beaches, vibrant coral reefs, and stunning sunrises.

Cuatro Islas — Digyo Island

⚲ Inopacan, Leyte

Digyo Island is the crown jewel of the Cuatro Islas group, boasting white sand beaches comparable to Boracay's famous shores. The four islands — Apid, Digyo, Mahaba, and Himokilan — are perfect for island-hopping adventures with pearl-white sand, crystal-clear water, and excellent snorkeling.

Canigao Island

⚲ Matalom, Leyte

A tropical jewel off Leyte's southwestern coast, Canigao Island features powdery white sand and crystal-clear waters teeming with vibrant marine life. The surrounding marine sanctuary makes it ideal for snorkeling, scuba diving, kayaking, and jet skiing. Don't miss the freshly caught seafood at the local wet market.

Sambawan Island

⚲ Maripipi, Biliran (near Leyte)

A small, stunning islet off Biliran's northwestern coast — formerly a sub-province of Leyte. Sambawan features pristine sandy stretches and a vibrant marine ecosystem perfect for divers. Trek to the island's highest point for a panoramic watchtower view, and stay for a magical sunset over the sea.

Limasawa Island

⚲ Southern Leyte

An idyllic seaside destination with deep historical significance — this is where Magellan held the first Christian mass in Asia in 1521. Beyond history, Limasawa's shores boast white sand beaches surrounded by crystal-clear waters. Fish sanctuaries make it ideal for snorkeling and scuba diving, with stunning views of Leyte, Bohol, and Mindanao.

Sabang Daguitan Beach

⚲ Dulag, Leyte

Leyte's go-to surf destination. Sitting at the coast of Dulag by the mouth of a river, this beach offers surfing, skimboarding, kayaking, and fishing. Enjoy easy river flow or catch ocean waves. The nearby surf camp offers floating huts on the river, rustic cabins, and even a pick-and-pay farm for a unique beach getaway.

Mahaba Island

⚲ Inopacan, Leyte

Part of the Cuatro Islas group, Mahaba Island lives up to its name ("long" in Filipino) with stretches of white sand shores and nearby marine sanctuaries housing diverse fish species. A quiet, unspoiled gem perfect for those who want a peaceful beach experience away from it all.

Red Beach (MacArthur Landing)

⚲ Palo, Leyte

One of the most historically significant beaches in the Philippines. On October 20, 1944, General Douglas MacArthur famously waded ashore here, fulfilling his promise "I shall return." Today, bronze statues commemorate the landing. The beach itself offers calm waters and a moving journey through World War II history.

Himokilan Island

⚲ Hindang, Leyte

The fourth island of the Cuatro Islas group, Himokilan features a lush green hill perfect for short treks alongside its powdery white shores. The surrounding waters offer beautiful coral formations and diverse marine life, making it a wonderful spot for snorkeling and quiet beach relaxation.

🌴 Visiting Leyte's Beaches

Fly into Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport in Tacloban City or take a ferry from Cebu to Ormoc City. Most beaches can be reached by boat from local ports in Palompon, Inopacan, Matalom, or Bato.

Best season: March to May (dry season). Always check local weather and sea conditions before island trips.
