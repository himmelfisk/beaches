Paradise in the Eastern Visayas

Leyte province, located in the heart of the Philippine archipelago, is home to some of the country's most stunning and unspoiled coastal destinations. With powdery white sand, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and vibrant marine life, these beaches offer a slice of tropical paradise far from the crowds.

Kalanggaman Island

⚲ Palompon, Leyte

One of the most photogenic islands in the Philippines, Kalanggaman is famous for its breathtaking 795-meter white sandbar that stretches out into crystal-clear waters. Named after the local word "langgam" (bird), its shape resembles a bird in flight. Recognized by Lonely Planet as one of the most picturesque islands in the Philippines, it's a must-visit destination with pristine beaches, vibrant coral reefs, and stunning sunrises.

Cuatro Islas — Digyo Island

⚲ Inopacan, Leyte

Digyo Island is the crown jewel of the Cuatro Islas group, boasting white sand beaches comparable to Boracay's famous shores. The four islands — Apid, Digyo, Mahaba, and Himokilan — are perfect for island-hopping adventures with pearl-white sand, crystal-clear water, and excellent snorkeling.

Canigao Island

⚲ Matalom, Leyte

A tropical jewel off Leyte's southwestern coast, Canigao Island features powdery white sand and crystal-clear waters teeming with vibrant marine life. The surrounding marine sanctuary makes it ideal for snorkeling, scuba diving, kayaking, and jet skiing. Don't miss the freshly caught seafood at the local wet market.

Sambawan Island

⚲ Maripipi, Biliran (near Leyte)

A small, stunning islet off Biliran's northwestern coast — formerly a sub-province of Leyte. Sambawan features pristine sandy stretches and a vibrant marine ecosystem perfect for divers. Trek to the island's highest point for a panoramic watchtower view, and stay for a magical sunset over the sea.

Limasawa Island

⚲ Southern Leyte

An idyllic seaside destination with deep historical significance — this is where Magellan held the first Christian mass in Asia in 1521. Beyond history, Limasawa's shores boast white sand beaches surrounded by crystal-clear waters. Fish sanctuaries make it ideal for snorkeling and scuba diving, with stunning views of Leyte, Bohol, and Mindanao.

Sabang Daguitan Beach

⚲ Dulag, Leyte

Leyte's go-to surf destination. Sitting at the coast of Dulag by the mouth of a river, this beach offers surfing, skimboarding, kayaking, and fishing. Enjoy easy river flow or catch ocean waves. The nearby surf camp offers floating huts on the river, rustic cabins, and even a pick-and-pay farm for a unique beach getaway.

Red Beach (MacArthur Landing)

⚲ Palo, Leyte

One of the most historically significant beaches in the Philippines. On October 20, 1944, General Douglas MacArthur famously waded ashore here, fulfilling his promise "I shall return." Today, bronze statues commemorate the landing. The beach itself offers calm waters and a moving journey through World War II history.

Tanauan Coast

⚲ Tanauan, Leyte

Known as the “Skimboarding Capital of the Philippines,” Tanauan's coastline along San Pedro Bay has hosted competitive skimboarding events since 2002. The wide, flat shoreline with consistent shallow waves creates ideal conditions for this thrilling water sport, attracting boarders from across the country.

Mayorga Beach

⚲ Mayorga, Leyte

Tucked along Leyte's eastern coast, Mayorga Beach is a quiet seaside stretch where locals gather at sunset while fishermen bring in their daily catch. With no resorts or crowds, it offers an authentic glimpse of coastal village life and a peaceful place to watch the horizon where the Pacific meets San Pedro Bay.

Baybay Beach

⚲ Baybay, Leyte

The coastal promenade of Baybay City stretches along the Camotes Sea on Leyte's western shore, backed by the vibrant university town that is home to the Visayas State University. The beach area comes alive at dusk with food stalls, students, and families enjoying the breezy waterfront and dramatic sunsets over the sea.

Calubian Coast

⚲ Calubian, Leyte

At the far northern tip of Leyte, the coastal town of Calubian rewards the adventurous with spectacular sunsets that few tourists ever witness. Facing the San Bernardino Strait with views toward Samar, the quiet waterfront and surrounding mangrove areas offer a remote, untouched corner of Leyte that feels worlds away from everything.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit beaches in Leyte?
The best time to visit Leyte's beaches is during the dry season from March to May, when you can expect sunny skies and calm seas. December to February is also pleasant but may see occasional rain. The typhoon season runs from June to November, so it's best to avoid island-hopping trips during that period.
How do I get to Leyte, Philippines?
You can fly into Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport (TAC) in Tacloban City, which has daily flights from Manila and Cebu. Alternatively, take a ferry from Cebu City to Ormoc City (about 4–5 hours). From Tacloban or Ormoc, you can take local buses, vans, or hire private transport to reach the various coastal towns and boat launch points.
Which is the most popular beach in Leyte?
Kalanggaman Island in Palompon is widely regarded as the most popular beach destination in Leyte. It is famous for its stunning 795-meter white sandbar, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant coral reefs. Lonely Planet has recognized it as one of the most picturesque islands in the Philippines.
Is it safe to swim at Leyte's beaches?
Most of Leyte's beaches are generally safe for swimming, especially during the dry season. However, always check local conditions before entering the water. Some island beaches like Kalanggaman and Canigao have designated swimming areas. For surf beaches like Sabang Daguitan, beginners should stay in shallow areas or take lessons from local instructors.
How much does it cost to visit Kalanggaman Island?
A day trip to Kalanggaman Island typically costs around PHP 1,500–3,000 per person (approximately USD 27–55), which includes boat transfer from Palompon, environmental fees, and basic facilities. Overnight camping is also available with additional fees. Group tours may be cheaper per person. Prices can vary by season.
Can I go island hopping in Leyte?
Yes! The Cuatro Islas group — Digyo, Mahaba, Himokilan, and Apid — is one of the best island-hopping destinations in the Visayas. Boats depart from Inopacan and you can visit multiple islands in a single day. Other popular multi-island trips include combining Canigao Island with Limasawa Island from the southern coast.
Are there historical beaches to visit in Leyte?
Yes! Red Beach in Palo is one of the most historically significant beaches in the Philippines. It's the site where General Douglas MacArthur waded ashore on October 20, 1944, fulfilling his famous "I shall return" promise during World War II. The MacArthur Leyte Landing Memorial with life-size bronze statues marks this historic event. Limasawa Island is also historically significant as the site of the first Christian mass in the Philippines in 1521.

🌴 Visiting Leyte's Beaches

Fly into Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport in Tacloban City or take a ferry from Cebu to Ormoc City. Most beaches can be reached by boat from local ports in Palompon, Inopacan, Matalom, or Bato. Coastal towns like Tanauan, Mayorga, Baybay, and Calubian are accessible by bus or van from Tacloban or Ormoc.

Best season: March to May (dry season). Always check local weather and sea conditions before island trips.
